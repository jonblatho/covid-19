Methodology

  • Most data here comes directly from reports published on most non-holiday weekdays by the Howell County Health Department (HCHD). To fill in gaps between those reports, when there is a substantial difference through the gap, I apply a linear equation to estimate the missing data. Where sources are available, those are linked in the table.
  • Sometimes testing data is missing from a report that is published. When that happens, I take the 7-day average of new tests conducted daily and use that to estimate testing data until reporting resumes. Once it does resume, I recalculate the estimates using the method mentioned in the above point.
  • I use HCHD data because, although the Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services (DHSS) updates its data more often/consistently, DHSS data for Howell County is lagging woefully behind HCHD’s reports for some reason.
  • While HCHD reports the total of active cases for the county, data for active cases by town is estimated. This is done by normalizing the 7-day total new cases for the county and categories (cities and “other”) and multiplying the county total active cases by those normalized values. Rounding may cause the sum of the by-town values not to exactly equal the county total.
  • This page includes both official data and unofficial estimates. For official data, please consult the Howell County Health Department.

Update schedule

Data should be updated by the following times as long as HCHD publishes the requisite data:

  • Monday–Thursday data: Most data by about 1 AM the following day, testing data by about 11 AM the following day
  • Friday–Sunday data: Most data by about 1 AM the following day, testing data by about 11 AM the following Monday
  • DHSS case totals: Updated every 1–2 days.

Missing data

  • HCHD did not begin providing an active case count until April 23, 2020.
  • Testing data isn’t available prior to August 19, 2020. That’s when HCHD began consistently publishing the number of SARS-CoV-2 tests conducted, rather than the number of individuals tested. For consistency, I opted to neither include nor estimate testing data before that date.
